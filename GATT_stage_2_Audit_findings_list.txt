Root cause analysis and root cause(s):	Cause Analysis:
The respective GATT CAPA process is described in SOP-QA-015 Measurement Analysis Improvement – CAPA, Version 3. The targeted date of corrective action implementation is set by the procedure (clause 6.4) to 90 days (monitored also as KPI). However, the verification (resp. monitoring of effectiveness) period is not considered. Because statistical data to verify effectiveness of implemented corrective actions to be gathered over a specific period, this process takes often much longer than the targeted 90 days value. Moreover, the procedure SOP-QA-015 does not limit overall / total period for closing CAPA (eg 1 year).
Long-term CAPAs (such as CAPA22006) were reviewed and CAPA owners interviewed, together with other CAPA meeting participants.
NCR/QA meetings take place every week; CAPA meetings are planned for every 6 weeks, but usually as a high-level meetings (with General Manager and DSCO as participants), with the aim to provide update on status all GATT QMS processes in CAPA status. These meetings are not usually focused on details and real processing of the certain CAPAs. Thus, the CAPA tasks requiring the same process approach as NCRs are not considered – resulting in ad hoc meetings and delays in completion.
Also, effectiveness of NCR or CAPA meetings is compromised in case that too many NCRs/CAPAs are reviewed at one NCR and CAPA meeting, without appropriate focus on chosen priorities, including smaller core teams relevant for the specific NCR / CAPA.
Sometimes, CAPAs tasks and corrective action defined broadly, not always with a reasonable limitation on scope, resulting into unrealistic timelines.
From CAPA (as well as NCR) processing point of view, NCR/CAPA owners miss unified root cause analysis tools that would help fasten the failure investigation process. Thus, the CAPA meetings are not fully effective because of choice of participants, tools used, frequency and tasks definition. The pending completion of CAPA tasks was opened during several consecutive Management Reviews (MR8, MR9, MR10).
Cause(s):
Processing of CAPAs is not fully developed (including definition of final due dates), unified and necessary tools are not properly used by GATT team.

Root cause analysis and root cause(s):	Cause Analysis:
The animal origin raw material fibrous gelatin sheet, used as component during production of GATT-Patch, is supplied by Gelita Medical GmbH.
The respective sections of MDR and series of the standard ISO 224422 specify regulatory requirements on control of risks related to animal origin materials used in medical devices. Based on the investigation, those requirements may impact the following processes, related procedures and quality records within GATT QMS:
Purchasing, Supplier Control and Audit processes: SOP-SC-001 Purchasing, Version 4, – purchase order requirements on animal origin materials
SOP-SC-002 Supplier Selection and Assessment, Version 3, – requirements on animal origin material supplier and requirements on consultants for AO area
SOP-QA-008 Audit, Version 6, – requirements on auditing of AO material suppliers Risk Management process:
SOP-QA-004 Risk Management Procedure, Version 4, and SOP-QA-014 Clinical Evaluation and PMSF, Version 2, – definition of GATT approach to risk assessment process related to animal origin materials
DHF-01-SFT-157 GATT-Patch Risk Management Report Animal Origin, Version 3, – regular report on identified risks and their mitigation Training process
SOP-RM-001 Training, Version 6, – requirements on training for personnel in contact with AO type of materials. However, the respective GATT procedures that concern animal origin raw material are not specific enough to capture all the ISO 22442 requirements. Moreover, the supplier of AO material, Gelita Medical GmbH, was not fully cooperative in providing necessary information as the mutual Quality Agreement signed with the supplier was not exact on details of information to be provided.
The above facts resulted into situation where animal origin material risk file DHF-01-SFT-157 was created using incomplete information. (Note: the supplier currently works on new version of risk assessment of their manufacturing process).
Also, GATT uses external experts for support in the risk assessments, but the CVs of involved experts were not properly reviewed for ISO22442 competence.
There is no sufficient awareness within broader GATT team regarding ISO 22442 as this standard is absent in the GATT annual training plan.
Cause(s):
Gaps in GATT procedures caused by combination of the following aspects: underestimation of ISO 22442 implementation process, lack of the supplier cooperativeness and insufficient training of GATT staff.

A documented system shall be established and maintained to control the quality of materials of animal origin and shall be verified by the medical device manufacturer. …Compliance is checked by inspection of the appropriate documents, including:
… the geographical origin (such as country or region) of the animal material, … including full traceability to the slaughterhouse.
The geographical origin can include the animal’s place of birth and the countries or regions in which it has lived during its lifetime as well as its place of slaughter. It is advisable that the manufacturer document the extent to which the geographical origin of the animal can be traced taking into account the application of risk management (see ISO 22442-1);
hygiene and quality assurance requirements to be met by the slaughterer including the provisions in the slaughterhouse to prevent cross-contamination within and between animals;
Handling
If any material of animal origin requires further dissection or trimming, it shall be removed as soon as possible to an area separate from that used for slaughtering and collection. This area shall be suitably equipped and maintained at an appropriate level of cleanliness and environmental protection. Implements for dissection and trimming shall be kept clean to minimize risk of cross-contamination. Ideally, a dedicated set of tools should be used for trimming and kept separate from the ones used for harvesting.
Source materials to be utilized in medical devices shall be segregated for delivery according to a documented procedure.
The manufacturer shall be responsible for ensuring that the handling of the material is conducted in accordance with the documented procedures.
5.4 Inspection
Sourcing of animal material shall be subject to control and individual inspection by a veterinarian.
There will, however, be some source-species where this is not possible (e.g. fish, crustaceans, cervids, mink, cat). If individual animals cannot be inspected, the justification for this shall be documented and a relevant sampling plan provided.
